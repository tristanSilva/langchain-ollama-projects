# Project Apollo: Quantum Computing Expansion Initiative
Date: October 24, 2025
Author: Dr. Elena Vance, Lead Research Director
Classification: Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Project Executive Summary
Project Apollo aims to scale our existing quantum annealers from 500 qubits to a stable 2,048-qubit architecture by Q4 2026. This hardware expansion will drastically minimize error rates in quantum superposition phases and solve optimization problems that are currently impossible for classical supercomputers.

## 2. Key Technical Infrastructure
Our core engineering strategy relies on three main technical pillars:
* Cryogenic Cooling Systems: Upgrading our dilution refrigerators to maintain a continuous baseline operating temperature of 15 millikelvins.
* Superconducting Qubits: Switching from traditional aluminum-based loops to a proprietary niobium-diselenide alloy to prolong coherence times.
* Error Correction Software: Implementing surface code error tracking to intercept and fix phase-flip anomalies before they corrupt active calculations.

## 3. Anticipated Risk Matrices
The engineering team has identified two major risks during the deployment phase:
1. Thermal Leakage: Minor fluctuations in ambient lab temperature could break the superconducting state, halting active operations.
2. Cross-Talk Interference: High qubit density naturally increases electromagnetic cross-talk. We will resolve this by inserting custom-fabricated metamaterial shielding shields between qubit rows.
